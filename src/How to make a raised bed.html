How to make a raised bed

Posted by Jalini Jose on May 16, 2023

Raised beds are a popular and effective way to create a thriving vegetable or herb garden. They provide numerous benefits, such as better soil drainage, improved growth rates, and reduced weed growth. Here are some simple steps to follow to create your own raised bed.


Materials:

- Lumber boards (cedar or redwood are ideal) - Screws - Weed-blocking fabric - Compost - Soil


Tools:

- Measuring tape - Saw - Drill - Square - Shovel


Step 1:

Measure and Cut Your Boards

Using a measuring tape and saw, cut your lumber boards to your desired length. Depending on the size of your raised bed, you may need anywhere from 4 to 12 boards.


Step 2:

Assemble Your Frame

Using a drill and screws, assemble the boards into the shape of a rectangle (or square). Use a square to ensure the corners are at right angles.


Step 3:

Prep Your Location

Choose a location that receives at least 6 hours of sunlight a day and has access to water. Remove any grass or vegetation within the area and lay down weed-blocking fabric.


Step 4:

Fill Your Bed

Fill the bed with a mixture of high-quality soil and compost. Aim for at least 12 inches of soil depth.


Step 5:

Plant Your Garden

Once your bed is filled, it's time to plant your garden! Consider which plants will thrive in the amount of sunlight and soil nutrients available in your raised bed.
